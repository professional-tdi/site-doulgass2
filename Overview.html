Frederick Douglass

Born as a black slave and would later become a Aboloshinst, Author, and widely known public speaker.

In the Early days

Frederick Douglass was born into slavery from a black mother and white father. When he was eight years old his master sent him to Baltimore, Maryland, to live in the household of Hugh Auld. Auld’s wife taught Douglass to read and write. His first attempt at escaping slavery was discovered when he was 15.

Citation

Frederick being vocale

On July 5, 1852, Frederick Douglass presented a speech addressing Independence Day celebration and provoked the question, “What to the slave is the Fourth of July?” Douglass at times had traveled six months in a year to give lectures on abolition. He spoke at events commemorating the signing of the Declaration of Independence, held at the Corinthian Hall in Rochester, New York.
