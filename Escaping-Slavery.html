How He escaped

Frederick Douglass had attempted to escape slavery several times, Douglass had left Covey’s farm in 1838 through boarding a train to Havre de Grace, Maryland. He disguised himself as a sailor, but he needed to show proof that he was freed slave, and since he was a runaway slave who did not have any “free papers” he borrowed a seaman’s protection certificate that stated a sailor was a citizen of the U.S. Luckily, the train conductor did not look closely at the papers, and Douglass gained his passage to freedom. From there he had traveled through Delaware before arriving in New York at the safe house of abolitionist David Ruggles. 
Once settled New York, he sent for Anna Murray, a free person of color women from Baltimore. She had joined him and the two were married in September 1838.
